Deliverables · what you walk away with

The kit.

Eight artifacts. Every one production-ready. Your engineers import them, your LLMs consume them, your team ships with them on day one. This is the list. Not negotiable - it's what makes the system work.

01 system.css

Your complete design system as a single CSS file. Tokens for color, type, spacing, radius, shadows, motion, z-index. Components for nav, panels, cards, CTAs, forms, status indicators. Responsive breakpoints. Accessibility baked in. One import, entire product covered.

02 brand.md

Voice, palette, principles, typography rules, and an explicit avoid list. What to say, what never to say, what your brand sounds like when nobody is editing it. Readable by humans and machines.

03 manifest.json

Machine-readable map of your entire system. Every token, component, class, keyframe, breakpoint, and voice rule - structured so your toolchain, CI, and LLMs can consume it without parsing CSS.

04 CLAUDE.md

Agent instructions for your LLM. Session prompt, system rules, file layout, component inventory, override patterns, banned behaviors. Your AI ships on-brand every session without being told twice.

05 style-guide/

Living reference page generated from the CSS. Every token rendered, every component demonstrated, every variant visible. Auto-updates when the system changes. No stale wiki. The code is the doc.

06 overrides.css

Multi-tenant override architecture. Sub-brands, product lines, and client-facing surfaces load the base system and swap tokens via scoped wrapper classes. One OS, many faces. No forks.

07 toolchain/

Voice linter, design audit, manifest validator, pre-commit hooks, scaffold generator. The system enforces itself. Your team doesn't babysit brand compliance - the toolchain catches drift before it ships.

08 governance/

SDLC rules, architecture decisions, QA checklists, agent operating instructions. The organizational layer that tells your team and your AI how the system is maintained, extended, and deployed. Not philosophy - operational docs that run the shop.

How we work

Four rules. Everything else follows.

01
Signal over spectacle
Every pixel earns its place. If it doesn't help the user understand state, hierarchy, or next action - it gets cut.
02
Systems, not pages
We design the tokens, components, and rules that generate screens. Your third page should take ten minutes, not ten days.
03
Code is the deliverable
The system ships as CSS, not a PDF. Variables, not hex values. Components, not screenshots. No translation layer. No drift.
04
Human-readable intelligence
Executive, engineer, operator, customer - they all need to feel at home. Information-dense without being intimidating.
