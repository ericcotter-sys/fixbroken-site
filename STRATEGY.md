# Fixbroken Strategy

## Mission

Fixbroken is Eric Cotter's product strategy, AI execution, and enterprise solution studio.

Eric is a career product executive and former fractional CPO with 26 years of experience across enterprise SaaS, automotive technology, data products, attribution, fleet and mobility workflows, AI product strategy, UX, product commercialization, executive alignment, and complex product transformation.

The core idea behind Fixbroken is that Eric's accumulated product judgment has now been unlocked by modern AI development tools like Claude and Codex. For most of his career, even strong product ideas were constrained by traditional development bottlenecks: engineering capacity, cost, slow cycles, missed context, inconsistent execution, handoff gaps, interpretation problems, and organizational drag.

Fixbroken exists because that constraint has changed. Eric can now move faster from strategy to prototype, from product judgment to interface, from customer problem to working system, and from executive vision to enterprise-ready solution.

This is not a generic AI consulting agency. It is not "ask a code box to build AI magic." Fixbroken is built around the idea that AI tools are only valuable when directed by strong product judgment.

Eric's advantage is deep pattern recognition. He understands where products break: unclear value, bad workflow design, weak UX, poor commercialization, technical overbuild, executive misalignment, confusing demos, disconnected customer needs, and features that impress internally but fail in the market.

Fixbroken helps companies clarify what they are building, who it is for, why it matters, how it should work, how it should be experienced, and how it should be sold.

The agency works across product strategy, fractional CPO leadership, UX direction, AI product design, workflow design, prototyping, product storytelling, brand positioning, commercialization, internal product systems, and enterprise solution creation.

Ideal clients include AI startups, SaaS companies, founders, enterprise teams, mobility companies, automotive technology companies, data companies, investors, and operators who have real capability but need sharper product direction and faster execution.

A representative example is VUMO: helping unjam their vehicle walkaround experience by making the product more approachable, human, usable, credible, and sellable. The goal is not just a cleaner interface. The goal is to turn technical capability into a product customers can understand, trust, and buy.

### Core promise

Turn raw capability into clear, usable, enterprise-grade product.

### Tone

Serious, sharp, operator-led, commercially grounded. Avoid generic AI hype. Avoid innovation theater. Avoid language that makes Fixbroken sound like a prompt shop, design agency, or lightweight chatbot consultancy.

Fixbroken is senior product leadership with AI execution leverage.

## Q2 2026 Priorities

By June 30, 2026, Fixbroken needs to prove it is a real consulting and product execution business, not a portfolio site or AI hobby project.

### 1. Ship the agency hub

Fixbroken.ai must clearly explain what Fixbroken does, who it serves, why Eric is credible, what problems it solves, and how to start a conversation. The site should feel like a serious product executive with AI leverage, not a generic AI agency.

### 2. Convert VUMO into a flagship proof point

The VUMO walkaround work should become the first serious case study. Help VUMO turn a technical inspection concept into something more usable, approachable, human, credible, and sellable. By end of Q2 there should be sharper UX direction, clearer product language, a better commercial story, and evidence that Fixbroken made the product more viable.

### 3. Define the core Fixbroken offer

Fixbroken needs 2-3 clean engagement models:

- **Product Unjam Sprint**: diagnose and reshape a broken product, workflow, demo, or AI experience.
- **AI Product Execution Sprint**: move from idea to prototype, demo, or product surface using Claude, Codex, and Eric's product judgment.
- **Fractional CPO / Product Counterpart**: ongoing senior product leadership for founders, execs, and teams that need sharper product direction.

### 4. Build the operating system

Fixbroken needs repeatable internal methods: intake framework, product diagnosis template, UX review framework, AI build workflow, client deliverable structure, case study format, pricing bands, proposal template, and engagement agreement / LOI template.

## Out of Scope (Q2 2026)

### 1. Do not build a broad AI agency brand

Avoid generic "AI transformation" language. Fixbroken is not a full-service AI consultancy. It is product judgment, UX clarity, workflow design, and enterprise product execution.

### 2. Do not chase too many verticals

Stay focused on automotive technology, enterprise SaaS, AI product UX, mobility/fleet/inspection workflows, founder-led product messes, and technical products that need commercialization.

### 3. Do not build Fixbroken-owned SaaS products before the consulting engine is validated

Prototypes, labs, demos, and internal tools are fine. But Q2 is about revenue, proof, case studies, positioning, and client pull.

### 4. Do not over-polish the brand before selling

The brand needs to be credible enough to convert. Do not spend the quarter perfecting visual systems, microinteractions, naming, and experimental pages at the expense of sales and proof.

## Kill Criteria

### 1. Positioning failure

If serious buyers cannot explain what Fixbroken sells, the positioning is too abstract. Pivot toward one obvious wedge: Product Unjam Sprint for AI and technical products.

### 2. VUMO proof point fails

If VUMO does not become a credible proof point, do not force it. Use it as private learning and build a clearer public demo or synthetic case study.

### 3. AI execution leverage doesn't materialize

If Claude, Codex, and AI-assisted development do not materially compress delivery, reduce the emphasis on execution studio and shift toward senior product strategy, UX direction, and commercialization.

### 4. Wrong-shape demand

If clients only ask for low-value implementation work, the buyer or positioning is wrong. Fixbroken is not "build me a chatbot." It is product judgment, workflow clarity, and enterprise product leverage.

### 5. Pricing power failure

If the work cannot command premium pricing, the offer is too vague, too tactical, or too similar to cheap AI implementation. Reframe around executive outcomes: product viability, faster commercialization, better demos, clearer buyer understanding, reduced product confusion, and stronger product-market fit signal.
